How you can meditate

  • In the early phases of practice it is sufficient to meditate for relatively short periods: 5 and 10 minutes each day and later to extend this to 20-30 minutes.
  • As the body gets used to exercise so it will get used to meditation. Hence it will become easier to do. Eventually almost every activity can involve some meditative and centred state.

Getting ready:

  • Dress appropriately when meditating. Avoid wearing tight clothes, loosen your clothes and/or wear casual sport-type clothing when practising.
  • Create a suitable space to practise meditating. A place that is spacious and has a simple but appealing environment is most suitable. It ideally should be warm, not too hot or cold, in a quiet area, and free from any sudden interruptions, such as from telephones, people entering, unexpected noises. Once you become well practised you will not find such things intrusive and you will become less dependent on having an appropriate space.
  • Adopt a relaxed and comfortable posture: sitting in a chair with your back and neck supported, or lying down again with your back and neck supported, or if you are sufficiently flexible, sitting in a crossed leg, upright pose.

Action:

  • Now gently bring your awareness to your breathing
  • without effort attend to the regular rhythm, in…and out, .in…and out - allow it to ebb and flow, as you observe, allowing it to continue naturally and without effort.
  • As you do so, notice how your breathing becomes more gentle and quiet, become aware of the almost imperceptible pause between breathing in and breathing out.
  • A gentle emphasis on the ‘out-breath’ is often helpful to gaining a meditative state. This can naturally be let go of as you become more and more relaxed.
  • You can focus on a helpful word such as: “calm”, or “peaceful”, or “relax”, or “easy”, or “in / out”. Hear it in your head or say it out aloud in rhythmic time with your breathing. Or, you could use the word “peace” when you are breathing in and “full” when you are breathing out
  • Carry these words with you and in time they will help you to become relaxed and centred wherever you are, just by saying them to yourself during meditation practice.

Additional tips:

  • If it helps, you can imagine yourself sitting near a river or on a bridge watching a stream flowing along. Notice the colour of the water and the ripples on the surface, see and hear them. Picture any intrusive thoughts and images that come to you lying on petals or leaves of various sizes on the surface of the water. Watch as each one passes, floating away, see them as they become smaller and smaller until one by one they disappear.
  • So meditation will become for you a powerful way to manage states such as agitation, anxiety and irritability. This technique allows you, even when not meditating, to draw upon its calming influence by recalling key words that trigger calm. Hence you are better able to maintain a constant state of wellness than before.
